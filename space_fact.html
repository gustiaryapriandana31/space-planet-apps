Andromeda Galaxy

On March 10, 2024, the new moon can be utilized for stargazing. After sunset, look towards the northwest. The Andromeda Galaxy will be visible near the constellations of Andromeda and Cassiopeia. The Andromeda Galaxy is the closest galaxy to the Milky Way. It can be seen with the naked eye or with the aid of binoculars in a dark area far from city lights.

Total Solar Eclipse

The moon will pass directly between the Earth and the Sun on April 8, 2024, blocking the Sun completely and resulting in a total solar eclipse. Use solar viewing glasses or other eye protection to observe this phenomenon. During this event, the sky will become dark. The total solar eclipse of 2024 is expected to last longer than the one in 2017, with the longest duration of 4 minutes.

The oldest meteor shower

The Lyrid meteor shower will be visible from April 14 to 30, 2024, with its peak activity occurring on April 22-23, 2024. The best time to observe Lyrids is around April 23, 2024, early in the morning, if the weather permits. This is because the moon's full phase is expected to reach its maximum on that day, which could affect visibility due to the moon's light. The Lyrid meteor shower is the oldest known meteor shower that is still visible today, with records dating back to 687 SM. It originates from Comet C/1861 G1 Thatcher, which orbits the Sun every 415 years.

Fast, Bright, Perseid Meteor Shower Plus Fireballs

The Perseid meteor shower will be active from July 17 to August 24, 2024, with its peak expected to occur on August 12-13, 2024. Bright and fast-moving meteors make the Perseid meteor shower highly anticipated. At its peak, the shower can reach a rate of 100 meteors per hour, accompanied by a bonus of fireballs. Find a dark spot in the sky to see some Perseid meteors. Perseid will appear to radiate from the constellation Perseus. This shower originates from the debris trail of Comet Swift-Tuttle, a large comet that orbits the Sun every 1862 years.

Planets in Space

Four planets will be in opposition in 2024, namely Saturn, Neptune, Uranus, and Jupiter. In opposition, planets are positioned opposite the Sun in the sky, so they will be fully illuminated and appear brightest. This astro-photography moment has the potential to be captured with a lens. Saturn will move into opposition on September 8, 2024, Neptune on September 21, 2024, Uranus on November 17, 2024, and Jupiter on December 7, 2024.

Supermoon

A Supermoon, also known as a full moon at perigee, will appear 14% larger and 30% brighter compared to a full moon at apogee. Supermoons will occur on September 18, 2024, and October 17, 2024. The Moon orbits the Earth in an elliptical or oval shape, which means it moves from its closest point to humans (perigee) to its farthest point from humans (apogee). When the full moon or new moon is at the perigee point, a Supermoon occurs.

Seven Brothers Star Cluster

The Pleiades star cluster can be observed throughout November 2024. This open star cluster with bright blue light in the constellation of Taurus can be seen with the naked eye or with the aid of binoculars. The Pleiades star cluster is also known as the seven sisters because of its seven brightest stars that can be seen directly. However, it actually consists of more than 1,000 stars. The Pleiades star cluster is about 100 million years old, which means that dinosaurs were still alive when these stars formed.
